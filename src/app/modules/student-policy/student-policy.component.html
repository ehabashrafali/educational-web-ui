Terms and Conditions

Please read the following terms carefully. These policies are designed to maintain a respectful, organized, and effective learning environment for all students, parents, and teachers.

1. Attendance Policy

  • Both students and teachers must ensure punctual attendance. No delays are accepted.
  • Aim to be online 5–10 minutes before the scheduled class time.

2. Dress Code

  • Students must wear modest and appropriate attire befitting a Holy Qur’an class.
  • To maintain a respectful and effective learning environment, all female students are kindly requested to attend their lessons in a private space where no men are present.
  • Some of our female teachers wear the niqab (face veil) and, for religious reasons, can only turn on their cameras when no men are in the same room.
  • If this condition is not met, the teacher will keep her camera off during the lesson, which may affect the quality of the session.

3. Class Rescheduling (Student-Initiated)

Cancellation more than 6 hours before the lesson: Allowed. The student will not be charged for the lesson.
Cancellation between 6 and 2 hours before the lesson: The student must reschedule the lesson.
Cancellation less than 2 hours before the lesson: The lesson will be counted for the teacher, and the student will not receive a refund or a replacement lesson.

4. Monthly Classes & Hours

  • The number of classes or hours is agreed upon when the student enrolls.
  • Payments are calculated based on hourly rates.

5. Permanent Schedule Changes

For a permanent change in class day or time (e.g. due to a long holiday or new schedule), students or parents should notify administration before the start of the month.

6. Fee Inquiries

Any questions about fees should be directed to the management, not the teacher.

7. Payment Policy

  • At the end of each month student receives the invoice of the previous month.
  • Monthly payments are processed between the 1st and the 10th day of the following month.

9. Quarterly Reports

  • Parents receive a report every month to monitor their child’s progress.
  • Parents of students under 16 can access these reports via the system.

10. Privacy Policy

  • Crescent School protects the privacy of both students and teachers.
  • Personal data is strictly confidential; no third party has the right to access or use it.
  • Communication channels (email, phone, social media) must only go through administration — no direct sharing between teachers and students/parents.
  • Referrals or recommendations must go to administration—not directly to teachers.
  • Only a portion of the student’s fees goes to the teacher; the rest funds school operations, staff, taxes, subscriptions, and to ensure high-quality education.

11. Rating & Feedback

  • The Support Team will reach out after a few lessons to request feedback.
  • Students are encouraged to share concerns, inquiries, or complaints. Your feedback helps improve our services.

12. Public Holidays

According to the Prophet ﷺ, “Allah has given you something better than them, the day of Eid Adha and the day of Eid Fitr.”

Eid Adha: 5-day holiday
Eid Fitr: 4-day holiday

13. Leaving the Institute

  • Students planning to leave must notify management two weeks in advance, and provide a reason.
  • Management will attempt to resolve any issues before departure.
  • Once the teacher-student relationship ends, no private contact or teaching is allowed. This is considered a trust (Amanah) before God.
